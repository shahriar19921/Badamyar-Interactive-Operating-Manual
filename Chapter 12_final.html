CHAPTER 12 EXTRACTION WELLS & FLOWLINES – WELLHEADS MANIFOLDS

SECTION 1 INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

1.1.1 Purpose

Manifold by definition is equipment gathering fluids from different sources to be routed to a common identified system. In the occurrences, there are three differents manifolds in WP4;

  • The 6” Vent Manifold, gathering the gas vented from the 2” vent lines located on each wellhead flowlines, to be routed to the Vent Header and to the Closed Drain / Vent KO Drum D-0040.
  • The 8” Test Manifold, gathering the selected well’s fluid to be routed to the Wet Gas Meter.
  • The 14” Production Manifold, gathering all the well’s fluids to be routed to the Pig Launcher then to LCP Platform.

1.1.2 System Criticality

This system is critical for safe operations and business continuity of the YADANA complex.

1.1.3 Key components

With the exception of the 6” Vent Manifold which is equipped only with manual ball and globe valves, the two other manifolds, Test and Production are equipped with actuated on-off switching valves for each wellhead, allowing a quick segregation of the well to be tested. The switching valves are as follows:

  • Switching valves for wells to test:

            - XV-52023 for well 2
            - XV-53023 for well 3

SECTION 2 SYSTEM / EQUIPMENT DETAILED DESCRIPTION

2.1. SYSTEM DESCRIPTION

See Drawing
“Wellhead Platform – WP4-Wellhead Manifolds” - MM-YAD-91-HHIE-150105 - Rev 05

Hide image

Flow from the wells normally goes to the production header via the production manifold. Wells can be tested in wet gas meter by diverting flow to the test manifold. The switching valves can be switched, operated by operators from the HMI on the PCS Cabinet of the WP4 Instrument Shelter or from the QP2-CR

Only one test manifold valve can be opened at a time to allow only one production well to be tested in wet gas meter. Production and test manifold are interlocked to avoid both valves to be opened or closed at the same time. This means that a switching valve actually open to the production manifold (XV-5x024, where “x” is the well number) should be closed before opening the corresponding switching valve to the test manifold (XV-5x023), leading to an increase of pressure for short period of time in the corresponding well flowline.

3.1. CONTROL DESCRIPTION

3.1.1 Switching Valves Control logic

The interlock avoiding the opening of more than one well to test as well as the impossibility to have the production manifold and test manifold switching valves opened or closed in the same time for the same well is controlled by the PCS using the limit switches open and close of each valves.

3.1.2 Differential Pressure Control

The differential pressure transmitter PDIT-50001 is provided between production manifold and test manifold. The PDAH-50001 set at 1.5 Bar is used to alert the operator prior opening XV’s Valve to test manifold.

SECTION 4 OPERATING MODES

4.1. OPERATING MODES

The switching valves can be switched, operated by operators from the HMI on the PCS Cabinet of the WP4 Instrument Shelter or from the QP2-CR.

4.3. NORMAL OPERATIONS

The normal well test operations are performed from the QP2-CR except if the differential pressure alarm PDAH-50001 is activated, indicating a permanent depressurization of the test manifold, which has to be investigated (leak not detectable by the gas detectors, drain valve to Closed Drain system not fully closed or passing)
