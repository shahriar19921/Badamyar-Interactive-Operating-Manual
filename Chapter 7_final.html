CHAPTER 7 SAFETY & CRITICAL SYSTEM – ELECTRICAL SYSTEM AND UPS

SECTION 1 : INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

1.1.1 Purpose

In normal operations, the electrical power to WP4 platform is fed from the 6.6kV switchboard/MCC (SW-1-1-1) on PP via subsea cable to the step down transformer located on WP4 and 400V switchboard/MCC (SW-41-2 1) which will distribute power to various loads on WP4:

  • Lighting and small power DB (SWLM 41-3-1)
  • 230V AC UPS (UPS 41-3-1) for telecom with 2 hours autonomy time
  • 24V DC UPS (UPS 41-5-1) for Instrument with 90 min. autonomy
  • 24V DC / 230V AC UPS (NOCP-41-2-1 for Navaids, obstruction lights, foghorn with 96 hours autonomy
In case of shut-down of the normal power supply, a temporary diesel generator,U-0023 (400VAC,3ph, 50Hz) to be connected via power receptacle will be provided as back-up power supply of WP4.

The Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) systems provide a power supply during the transition to Temporary Power Supply, following loss of main power. They also safeguard personnel and maintain operation of safety equipment for specified periods in the event of total power failure or shutdown.

1.1.2 System Criticality

Electrical system and UPS are a support system critical to the normal, safe operation of the WP4.

1.1.3 Key components

On WP4 the Electrical and UPS Systems comprise:

  • 6.6Kv/420V 400KVA Power Transformer TR-41-2-1
  • LV Switch Rack SW-41-2-1
  • 400V / 230 V 24 KVA Transformer TR-41-3-1
  • Lighting and Small Power Distribution Board SWLM 41-3-1
  • 230V AC UPS (UPS 41-3-1) for telecom with 2 hours autonomy time
  • 230V AC Distribution board SW-41-3-1
  • 24V DC UPS (UPS 41-5-1) for Instrument with 90 min. autonomy
  • 24V DC Distribution Board SW-41-5-1 for PCS, ESD and F&G Cabinets
  • 24V DC / 230V AC UPS (NOCP-41-2-1 for Navaids, obstruction lights, foghorn with 96 hours autonomy
  • One inverter 24 / 230V and Distribution Board for the Main Lantern 1 to 4
  • One inverter 24 / 230V and Distribution Board for the Navigation Obstruction Lighting (Crane & Vent)
  • One inverter 24 / 230V and Distribution Board for the Fog Detector and For Horn at Main Deck
  • One inverter 24 / 230V and Distribution Board for the Helideck Status Light

1.1.4 Locations

Electrical and UPS equipment as mentioned in 1.1.3 are located on the Main Deck East Side

1.1.5 Interface with other Platforms

to WP4 platform is fed from the 6.6kV switchboard/MCC (SW-1-1-1) on PP via subsea cable.

2.1.2 Control Philosophy



The protective device of HV/LV Power Distribution system involves the intertrip signal from the upstream 6.6kV feeder circuit breaker (unit 7B1) on HV Switchboard/MCC extension on PP to the downstream

400V AC circuit breaker (Q1) of LV Switchrack (SW-41-2-1) on WP4 platform and vice versa via fiber optic cable through PSS on WP4 and ESD system on PP.

The tripping of the 6.6kV upstream feeder circuit breaker (unit 7B1) of HV Switchboard / MCC extension on PP will involve direct power shutdown on the downstream 400V AC circuit breaker (Q1) of LV Switchrack (SW-41-2-1) on WP4 platform.

The interlock device between 6.6kV/420V Power Transformer (TR-41-2-1) and upstream 6.6kV Switchboard/MCC (SW-1-1-1) extension unit (7B1) is operated via key interlock.

The opening / closing status, unavailable, common alarms and earth fault of upstream feeder circuit breaker (unit 7B1) of HV Switchboard / MCC (SW-1-1-1) extension on PP is monitored through PDC-1-4-1. The ESD signal (XS-49292) for the upstream feeder circuit breaker is initiated upon trip of PP safety bar ESD-0.06.

On WP4, the opening/closing status, unavailable and common alarms of incomer 400V AC circuit breaker (Q1) on LV Switchrack (SW-41-2-1) are monitored. The ESD signal (XS-58006) for the incomer circuit breaker (Q1) on LV Switchrack (SW-41-2-1) is initiated upon trip of WP4 safety bar ESD-1G.
