CHAPTER 4 : SAFETY & CRITICAL SYSTEM – FIRE & GAS SYSTEM

SECTION 1 : INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

1.1.1 Purpose

The Fire and Gas Detection System, as part of the Plant Safety System (PSS), is provided to detect abnormal concentration of flammable gas and the existence of fire on WP4 and activate related alarms and initiate executive actions (ESD activation, electrical isolation) so as to minimize the risk associated with fire and gas hazards. Early warning will be based on detection of flammable gas, fire and heat at various locations on the WP4 platform. The prime objective in the design of the fire and gas detection system is the reduction in escalation potential that may result from potential fire or explosion hazards. The system, under the appropriate fire and gas conditions, automatically raises alarms (audible and visual) and initiates the shutdown systems. The provision of a Gas Alarm system provides early warning to personnel on the platforms of the existence of a significant hazard and enables timely and orderly evacuation of personnel from the affected areas and ensures speedy fire control / extinction by manually operated fire extinguishing systems or appliances, to prevent or minimise escalation to equipment and structures. PSS process the Emergency Shutdown and the Fire and gas logics. Fire and Gas sensor loops shall be connected to the PSS marshalling cabinets and cross-wired to PSS input cards dedicated to the Fire & gas in the dedicated racks. The offshore facilities are divided into fire zones for the purpose of both detection and protection. Each Fire zone is divided into fire areas per geographical zones. Each fire area shall be covered by a group of fire and/or gas detectors. The Fire and Gas matrix panels and F&G mimics are presented on the basis of these zones. WP4 is a remote satellite wellhead platform. Thus, the requirement to define fire zone separations does not apply to this installation. Therefore, WP4 fire zone is considered a single fire zone. Combined visual/audible alarms are installed on WP4. General alarm (combined visual/audible alarms) is initiated manually via manual call points (MAC) or automatically upon confirmed gas or fire detection.

1.1.2 System Criticality
As it preserves the safety of the personnel and the integrity of the equipment, the fire and gas system is one of the most critical systems of the platform.

1.1.3 Key components
On WP4 the F&G system manages the following devices:

  • MAC’s
  • Audible and visible Alarms
  • IR Point Gas Detector
  • UV/IR Flame Detector
  • Fusible Plug
  • Heat Detector

1.1.4 Locations
Fire and gas detectors are located throughout the decks depending on the risks associated to the different zones except for the Weather Deck and the Surfer Landing.
For a detailed listing of the detectors with their locations, see section 2.1.2

1.1.5 System Dependencies Operation of the F&G system require the following to run

  • ICSS (see Chapter 6)
  • Electrical System and UPS (see Chapter 7)

SECTION 2 SYSTEM / EQUIPMENT DETAILED DESCRIPTION

2.1. FIRE AND GAS DETECTORS

2.1.1 Fire Detection

Fire detection is provided in the wellhead, production header and test header areas of WP4 by means of fusible plugs; which are pressurized by nitrogen, and UV/IR flame detectors.

Activation of low pressure alarm in fusible plug loop (2oo3 voting) initiates automatic actions. In addition, all outdoor process areas of WP4 platform are covered by UV/IR flame detectors. Executive action are initiated by 2ooN (N ≥ 3) voting of flame detectors.

The trip voting logic is as follows:

  • 2ooN: Detection confirm or
  • 1ooN: Detection confirm if N-1 Detector fault is confirmed or
  • N detector fault
Automatic safety actions based on fire detection in open areas of WP4 are:
  • General audio and visual alarms on WP4
  • Alarm in QP2 control room;
  • Alarm “Confirmed Fire” on F&G Matrix panel;
  • Initiate ESD-1 Fire, through ESD-1F
  • During drilling rig activities, activate visual and audible alarm (XS 50907 and 50908)

2.1.2 Gas Detection

Flammable gas detection is provided on WP4 covering all areas of potential sources of flammable gas or at the inlet of enclosures with electrical components not certified for Zone 1 hazardous area.

IR point type gas detectors are provided. They are calibrated for methane.
The detectors are, as far as possible, installed in locations where environmental effects will not influence their operation (rain, wind…)

Automatic executive actions will be initiated on confirmed high-high level gas detection by 2ooN (N≥3) voting of IR point type gas detectors.

Single high or high-high level gas detection at 20% LEL or 50% LEL respectively raises an alarm locally and in the QP2 control room.
Actions in case of confirmed high-high level gas detection in WP4 platform are;

  • General audio and visual alarms on WP4;
  • Alarm in QP2 Control room
  • Alarm “Confirmed Gas” on F&G Matrix panel;
  • Isolate and inhibit start of all electrical users not certified for Zone 0 or 1;
  • Initiate ESD-1G.
Actions in case of any gas detection alarm (20 % LEL from any gas detection) in WP4 platform are:
  • Activate helicopter status light to warn approaching helicopter.

2.1.3 Toxic Gas Detection

The level of H2S considered in the design is not sufficient to justify fixed H2S detection. Portable H2S detection devices should be provided for operations requiring pipeline or equipment intervention, where hydrocarbon leakage is possible.

It is not envisaged that non-invasive operations will require portable H2S detectors or that operators will routinely carry such items.

2.1.4 Manual Alarm Call Point (MAC’s)

Manual alarm call (MAC) points are provided at each level that contain a work area, close to each stair case, access way and at all bridge accessed area.
Manual alarm call point (MACs) are provided and strategically distributed throughout WP4 to enable personnel to raise an alarm in case of potential emergency situation. MACs are distributed along the access ways so that a MAC may be reached within 20 m of traveling distance as per GS EP SAF 312.
MACs shall be of the double action (type B) type and be colored red. MACs shall activate alarms to the QP2 control room and locally on WP4. MACs are certified for Zone 1 hazardous area.

2.1.5 ESD-1G Hand Switches

ESD-1G Hand Switches (push buttons) are placed at the strategic locations of WP4 in order to be accessed by escaping personnel in case of emergency situation.

  • Two (2) in cellar deck near stair way
  • Two (2) in mezzanine deck near stair way to helideck

ESD-1G push buttons are certified for Zone 1 hazardous area.
