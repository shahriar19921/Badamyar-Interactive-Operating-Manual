CHAPTER 2 : ESCAPE & EVACUATION FACILITIES & SAFETY EQUIPMENT

SECTION 1 : INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

        1.1.1 Purpose

The objectives of escape, evacuation and safety equipment and facilities are:
      •     to ensure the safety of personnel when they escape out of their workplace in case of emergency,
      •     to provide safe refuges on the installation for as long as required for a controlled evacuation,
      •     to ensure safe evacuation of the installation,
      •     to facilitate rescue of personnel in distress and give treatment to the injured.
The escape, evacuation and safety equipment has been designed for a maximum POB of 12 persons.


         1.1.2 System Criticality

The availability of escape routes and evacuation and safety equipment is critical for the safety of all personnel on board.


         1.1.3 Key components

The escape and evacuation facilities and safety equipment consists mainly of:
      •     primary escape routes,
      •     secondary escape routes,
      •     Helicopter Rescue Box (2)
      •     Life Buoys with self-igniting light and self-activating smoke signal (8)
      •     Life Rafts 12 persons (2)
      •     Scramble Net (2)
      •     Life Jacket Container (2 for 15 persons)
      •     Life Jacket Container (2 for 3 persons)
      •     First Aid Box (1)
      •     Stretcher basket type (1)
      •     Muster Area (12 persons)
      •     Escape Hood Cabinet (12 set of escape Hood)
      •     Knotted Rope (2)
      •     EPIRB (2)
      •     Safety Shower (1)
      •     Eyewash (1)
      •     fire protection & fighting equipment,
      •     POB tracking system
      •     Fog horn
      •     Navaid

SECTION 2 SYSTEM / EQUIPMENT DETAILED DESCRIPTION

2.1 EVACUATION MEANS

Primary and secondary escape routes for WP4 are provided to facilitate personnel evacuation in critical situations.

Upon sounding of the GA to muster, personnel will proceed to the designated Primary Muster Point (PMP) on the WP4, which is located at the North-East corner of the Cellar Deck.

Primary Escape Route shall be at least 1.2 m wide, while the Secondary Escape Route shall be at least 0.8 m wide. No unobstructed height should be identified below at least 2.3 m.

Escape Routes are painted by yellow / black zebra lines, illuminated and fitted with anti-slip.


         2.1.1 Primary means

The primary means of evacuation will be by work boat which is the normal means of transfer of personnel to and from the wellhead platform to standby vessel or by Fast Rescue Boat from Stand-by vessel. helicopter may be used for emergency and medical evacuation purposes and on occasions when access to the platform by work boat is prevented by adverse weather.


         2.1.2 Secondary means

Secondary means of evacuation are not provided since the platform is not normally manned.


         2.1.3 Tertiary means

The tertiary means of evacuation will be by life rafts.

One 12-person life rafts 91 SK 0213-01 is available on the cellar deck at the North East corner and one 12-person life raft 91 SK 0213-02 is available on the cellar deck South side. These double-skinned orange rafts are encapsulated and self-inflating, designed to be thrown overboard.

Life jackets are available in life jacket cabinets in the immediate vicinity of life raft launching locations. They are stored in weather resistant storage boxes: one cabinet 91 SK 0207-01 with 15 life jackets and one cabinet 91 SK 0207-02 with 15 life jackets on the cellar deck. 2 cabinets with 3 life jackets are also provided at the surfer landing level

Life jackets are of color "high visibility orange". They have the name of the installation marked on them for identification and are fitted with whistle and safety light (8-hour autonomy).
