CHAPTER 3 : SAFETY & CRITICAL SYSTEM – SHUT-DOWN PHILOSOPHY

SECTION 1 : INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

        1.1.1 Purpose

The Wellhead Platform WP4 is provided with an Emergency Shut Down (ESD) system which, in emergency situation, reduces potential consequences of the situation by isolating flammable inventories and shutting down electrical equipment that could represent sources of ignition or releases of hydrocarbons. The Emergency Shutdown System (ESD) is an integral part of the Plant Safety System (PSS) in which the primary function is to automatically bring the facility to a safe state in the event of excursions beyond the normal design parameters of the process.

        1.1.2 System Criticality

This system is essential for the safe operation of WP4

        1.1.6 Interface with other platforms

ESD system operates in a fail safe mode, de-energized status of any digital or analogue sensor (input) and fault detection of any critical system component and field launches an action that bring the plant to the safe condition.

In normal conditions, Offshore Yadana facilities are controlled and monitored from the Central Control Room (CCR) located on the living quarters Platform (QP2) also called QP2-CR.

Emergency Shutdown of the WP4 platform, either manual or automatic will not cause shutdown of the Central Facility (MCP, PP) since gas from the other platforms will still be processed.

2.1. INITIATION OF SHUTDOWN

        2.1.1 Manual Initiation

Shutdown and/or blowdowns can be initiated manually by ESD push-buttons located near process units and access points or remotely from the control room ESD matrix panel.

An ESD matrix panel is included in the operator consoles of QP2 control room.


        2.1.2 Automatic Initiation

Shutdowns are designed to operate automatically when the process is outside normal operating limits and when a dangerous situation is likely to occur before an operator could intervene.

All automatic shutdowns are initiated only as a last resort and shall be preceded by PCS pre-alarms allowing the operators to take corrective actions.

Upon receipt of confirmed Fire or/and Gas signals from the F&G logic, part of the PSS application, shall also automatically initiate shutdown.


        2.1.3 ESD Override Functions

ESD override functions are accessed through the PSS maintenance console based on Intouch software.

Events are logged and printed to an associated printer. In normal operating conditions, both console and printers are in QP2 Instrument Technical Room.


        2.1.4 Start-up Override

In order to be able to start up equipment or sections of process, it shall be necessary to override some inputs to the ESD system, as sensor signal may be in abnormal state prior to start up and could cause a shutdown.

Such overrides are designated as “start-up overrides”.

        2.1.5 ESD Maintenance Override

Isolate and inhibit functions are available to allow testing of the ESD system loops and ESD logic during maintenance operation. Access to these facilities shall be via the PSS maintenance console under password control. These facilities are not available via the PCS, however the PCS shall display the status of all such isolate and inhibit functions.

“Inhibit” shall only segregate an input from the associated application logic allowing the input to go into an active position without performing a trip or executive action as the logic is put to a healthy condition.

Similarly, “Isolate” shall only segregate an output from the associated executive application logic maintaining the output in a healthy condition irrelevant of any shutdown causes. Individual override status indication shall be provided with alarm, at the PCS & PSS maintenance console and on event log print-out to ensure that no overrides are permanently left engaged.


        2.1.6 ESD Reset and Restart Functions

Reset facilities are available from the operator consoles to reset latched ESD application logics in sequence once the shutdown initiating input has returned to a normal condition.

Manual restart authorisation facilities is provided at the operator station. These restart facilities may be dedicated to a single output or may be common to number of outputs as required for safe operation of the plant.

Attempts to operate the restart authorisation facility is defeated when a trip request is still active, or when a local permissive reset is not initiated.

After a shutdown, the field operator shall authorise the opening of the valve locally using a reset push-button on the ESD valve panel, this will allow the operator in control room to open the ESD valve.
