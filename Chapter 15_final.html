CHAPTER 15 UTILITIES – OPEN DRAINS

SECTION 1 INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

1.1.1 Purpose

The open drain system receives drains from atmospheric sources such as deck drains, drip pans, and equipment tundishes.
The system consists of 3 main types of open drains:

  • OD1: continuously oil-contaminated hazardous area open drain header.
  • OD2: accidentally oil-contaminated hazardous area open drain header.
  • OD3: oil-free drains.

    • Hide image

    Hide image

OD1 are routed to the open drain drum (D-0041)
OD2 are routed to disposal tube (D-0042).
OD3 are discharged directly overboard to sea

1.1.2 System Criticality

The availability of the open drains system is not directly essential to maintain production until there is maintenance operations.

The open drains system is an integral part of the WP4 platform. Although not physically linked to any main processes, it is a vital part of the infrastructure in safely disposing of liquids away from equipment that may be being prepared for maintenance, repaired or recently reinstated.

The importance of containing potential hazardous liquids is crucial to prevent the possibility of potentially hazardous material settling under equipment or on any decks. Materials such as, hydraulic oil, methanol, condensate any chemical or danger of fumes from lighter hydrocarbons used throughout the platform may be prone to ignition, if there is any ignition source work on going in that particular area. The OD1 network facilitates the collection of these materials.

1.1.3 Key components

The system comprises the following main pieces of equipment:

  • OD1: continuously oil-contaminated hazardous area open drain header.
  • OD2: accidentally oil-contaminated hazardous area open drain header.
  • OD3: oil-free drains which in fact is divided in 2 parts
    •         - 6”-OD-1448-0-B91 with its own overboard to sea at EL 8.0 m
            - 6”-OD-1447-0-B91 with its own overboard to sea as well at EL 8.0 m
  • Disposal Tube D-0042
  • Open Drain Drum D-0041

2.1.1 System Description

The open drain system receives drains from atmospheric sources such as deck drains, drip pans, and equipment tundishes.

The open drains from deck drains on weather deck are discharged directly overboard to sea (OD3). The open drains from chemical laydown area, safety shower and transformer areas are connected to accidentally oil-contaminated hazardous area open drain header (OD2). The open drains from pig launcher drip pan, HPU2 tundish, wet gas meter drip pan, wellhead manifold area, wellhead control panel drip pan, corrosion inhibitor skid drip pan and crane drip pan are connected to continuously oil-contaminated hazardous area open drain header (OD1).

The hazardous area open drains (OD1) are routed to the open drain drum (D-0041) and hazardous area open drains (OD2) are routed to disposal tube (D-0042). Independent OD1 and OD2 open drain headers are provided to prevent migration of hydrocarbon.

Each OD1 and OD2 connection is provided with a U-seal and connected to top of header at 45 degree. This U-seal provides a liquid entry seal designed to keep air out of the drain system that could potentially cause an explosive mixture in the headers. It also prevents the release of any hydrocarbon vapors that may be migrating through the drain system.

Tundishes are used when single drain sources or multiple small drain lines need to be drained and there is no convenient way of directing these to a larger collection point. A drip pan is generally used underneath equipment to collect spills. All entries to the drains are protected from the ingress of solids by grating or drain box caps. Should solids make their way into the drain and block the U-seals, these have been designed to be easily roddable and with a flanged nozzle at bottom of the U-seal.

All open drain lines are provided with a minimum slope of 1:100 and the open drain headers have rodding out connections.

The open drain piping material is GRP. There is no service water system on WP4 but a washdown rate of 20 m3/h has been conservatively assumed to account for any portable/temporary wash water arrangement.

The open drain system (open drain drum, open drain header and disposal tube) is designed based on a washdown rate of 20 m3/h (no effluent on the platform thus 0 m3/h for effluent rate). Rainwater, accidental spillage and firewater are not considered as governing cases.

The Open Drain Drum D-0041 overflows to the disposal tube D-0042 (caisson) via a minimum 600 mm liquid seal. The height of the loop seal determines the operating level of the Open Drain Drum D-0041 i.e 800 mm from the vessel bottom. It also has the means of disposing of hydrocarbon liquids that may build up in the vessel.

There is no dedicated pump for the Open Drain Drum D-0041 to dispose of hydrocarbon liquids. Instead, hydrocarbon liquids will be drained manually by gravity to a supply boat. A portable pump is to be installed to recover any hydrocarbon from disposal tube. This pump will be located on and operated from the Supply boat.

The open drain drum D-0041 and disposal tube D-0042 have a device at the top to introduce a batch of biocide injection to prevent micro-organism growth.

2.1.4 Open Drain Drum D-0041

Hide image

The purpose of the Open Drain Drum D-0041 is to remove (and capture for recovery) oil droplets (above 500 μm). This shall be based on gravity settling without any special internals except an oil skimmer pipe equipped with vortex breaker connected to the Surfer Landing Supply Boat disposal via a flexible line

The Open Drain Drum D-0041 is made of carbon steel with 3 mm CA. The drum is provided with internal coating and sacrificial anodes for protection against corrosion. The drum dimension is 4,300 mm T/T with an inside diameter of 1,400 mm. The drum is a horizontal two-phase separator.

Open Drain Drum is vented to safe location and equipped with flame arrester and bird screen Design pressure and temperature are 3.5 barg and 64°C max / 0°C min.

2.1.5 Disposal Tube D-0042

Hide image

The overflow from Open Drain Drum D-0041, maintenance drains from closed drain/vent K.O. drum and pump drip pan, hazardous open drains (OD2), and drains from surfer landing drip pan are sent to the Disposal Tube D-0042 where the water is disposed to sea. The caisson is not part of the HC/water separation system and is used to dispose of the water to sea at an elevation sufficiently below LAT that surface contamination does not occur.

In the event of a major upset resulting in a large volume of HC passing to the caisson the hydrocarbons can be skimmed and pumped out using a portable pump mounted on a supply/work boat. A permanent flexible floating suction is installed in the caisson and connected to the caisson wall above the LAT. The suction line is run from the side of the caisson (nozzle “O2” is located less than 6 m above LAT) to the boat mooring where it can be connected by flexible hose to the pump located on the boat. The disposal tube D-0042 is made of carbon steel with 10 mm CA and provided with internal coating and sacrificial anodes.

The Disposal Tube D-0042 length is 24,560 mm (without the cover) with an inside diameter of 722 mm. Its design pressure and temperature are ATM and 64°C max / 0°C min.

The Disposal Tube is equipped with vent and flame arrester to atmosphere.

3.1. CONTROL DESCRIPTION

3.1.1 Disposal Tube Level Control

HC liquids are separated by gravity in the disposal tube and accumulates above the water level. The level transmitter LIT-50036 monitors the liquid top level in the disposal tube D-0042. Upon sensing a liquid top level at 100 mm above HAT, high alarm LIAH-50036 is generated to alert the operator. The hydrocarbons inside the Disposal Tube D-0042 can be removed by the supply boat when it visits WP4.
