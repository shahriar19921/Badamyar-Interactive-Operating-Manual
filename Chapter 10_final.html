SECTION 1 INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

1.1.1 Purpose

The Hydraulic Power Unit 2 U-0044 is in charge of controlling the opening and closing of the following valves on WP4 via MP and LP Headers:

MP Header:

  • Choke Valves ( PV-51005 ,PV-52005 , PV-53005 , PV-54005 ).

1.1.2 System Criticality

The Hydraulic Power Unit 2 is essential for the well’s production, the protection of personnel and equipment on the WP4 wells. Without this system the function of the platform would be inoperable.

1.1.3 Key components

The Hydraulic Power Unit 2 U-0044 is equipped with the main following components:

  • MP Hydraulic Pumps P-0044 A/B driven by electric motors.
  • MP manual Hydraulic Pump P-0044 C provided as back-up.
  • Reserve and Return Hydraulic Oil Tank (T-0044).
  • MP supply and return header
  • LP supply and return header
  • One set of four 54 L accumulators for the MP header.
  • One set of two 5 L accumulators for the LP header.
  • Suction Strainers at the suction of each Pump
  • Dual filters (Max. 5 micron), with manual change over, purge valve, local clogging indicator and clogging alarm are installed downstream of the pumps.

SECTION 2 SYSTEM / EQUIPMENT DETAILED DESCRIPTION

2.1. EQUIPMENT DESCRIPTION

See Drawing
"Hydraulic Power Unit– P&ID for WP4–U-0044 - MM-YAD-36-HH19-422056 - Rev 05

2.1.1 Hydraulic Pump P-0044A/B, (MP)

The HPU2 is equipped with one set of two hydraulic pumps P-0044 (radial piston type) in duty/standby mode (2x100%). The hydraulic pumps are driven by electric motors. The local control for the electric motor is performed by two start/stop push buttons. One emergency stop push button is provided for each pump. The local controls are installed on the front face of the HPU.

The local/remote and duty/standby selectors are provided in the Process Control System (PCS). The pumps are connected to the hydraulic tank and a suction strainer is installed between the tank and pump. The discharge line is equipped with a discharge header, non-return valves and dual filters (Max. 5 micron), with manual change over, purge valve, local clogging indicator and clogging alarm.

The pumps are capable of running at the maximum working pressure of the valve actuators.

The pumps are sized to fully charge accumulators:

  • from zero (0 barg) to minimum pressure (85 barg) within 3.78 minutes.
  • from the minimum pressure (85 barg) to the maximum operating pressure (110 barg) within 5.83 minutes.
A Hand Pump (P-0044C) is supplied to back up the main hydraulic generation system.

2.1.2 Reserve and Return Tank T-0044

The main reservoir (reserve tank) is sized to contain 150% of the complete cold fill of the hydraulic system, including the volumes of the valve actuators, hydraulic accumulators, headers and hydraulic lines to valves. The Main reservoir oil volume is 642.8 liters. The filling system is provided with a captive plug and filter. The filter shall be easy accessible for maintenance. The hydraulic oil used is Total Equivis ZS 15 or equivalent.

The oil return reservoir is sized to contain 150% of the total volume of all valves actuators. Return reservoir oil volume is 54.9 liters. The tank comprises of a settlement chamber, which receives the return fluid separated from the main reservoir by a weir. An outlet pipe from the return chamber is connected to the reserve tank through a filter to allow fluid to return to the reserve tank via gravity flow. The filter is isolatable for ease of maintenance.

Each tank (reserve tank and return tank) are equipped with a flame breather arrestor.

2.1.3 Accumulators

A set of 4 x 54 L accumulators for the MP Header and a set of 2 x 5 L accumulators for the LP Header are provided in a separate rack outside HPU. They are pneumatic bladder type inside the accumulator bottle with nitrogen charging medium. A nitrogen refilling system (charging kit & booster pump) is provided in HPU to recharge the nitrogen to 76.5 barg for MP Supply Header & 3 barg for LP Supply header. The accumulator are fitted with double block and bleed valves on the oil side.

The accumulator is also fitted with isolation, drain and relief valves on the oil side to permit replacement without causing a complete system shutdown.

The tank is equipped with a flame arrestor breather IP 45.

3.1 CONTROL DESCRIPTION

3.1.1 MP Hydraulic Header

The MP hydraulic Header is pressurized by the Hydraulic Pump P-0044 A and B, driven electrically in duty/standby mode (2x100%). A manual pump P-0044 C is provided as an emergency back-up.

The pumps can be operated in local by two start/stop pushbuttons and an emergency stop push button installed on the front face of the Unit

QP2-CR Operator can control the pumps from DCS or WP4 Operator from HMI when the local/remote selector is in remote selection. The local/remote, duty/standby selector is selectable by operator from DCS or from HMI.

When duty Pumps P-0044A/B is in automatic remote mode, the hydraulic pump stops at 110 barg and starts when the pressure drops to 90 barg. PIAH-50058 High pressure Alarm activates at 115 barg and low pressure alarm PIAL-50058 activates at 85 barg.

The stand-by pump, will start only in case of fault on the duty pump or when the operator will switch the stand-by pump to duty.

SECTION 4 OPERATING MODES

4.1. EQUIPMENT OPERATING MODES

There are two operating mode for the Hydraulic Pumps P-0042, P-0043 and P-0045:

  • Local from the WHCP front side
  • Remote from the HMI of the Well Control Module or from the PCS in QP2 Control Room.
The local control for the electric pumps is performed by:
  • Two start/stop pushbuttons.
  • One emergency stop pushbutton
The remote control from QP2- CR allows the following selection:
  • Local / remote
  • Duty / Stand-by
The DHSV, UMV and WV valves opening is manual from WHCP front face of the drawer, well by well, valve by valve the following opening sequence:
  • DHSV - 1st
  • UMV - 2nd
  • WV - 3rd
The valve closing sequence is either on command from the platform control system or locally from WHCP. If operated locally that is valve by valve local operate, following closing sequence automatically hydraulically performed.
  • WV - 1st
  • UMV - 2nd
  • DHSV - 3rd
Action on wellhead platform valves from QP2 is via solenoid valves (de-energized to trip) located inside the WHCP cabinet (XY-50065 for ESD-1, XY-50066 for ESD-2, and XY-5X018 for ESD3.1X).

4.2. NORMAL START-UP

The normal start-up of the Wellhead Control Panel will be done locally with all pumps in “local position” and the wells valves opened locally from the WHCP.

4.3. NORMAL OPERATIONS

The normal operations of the Hydraulic Pumps after start-up should be from the DCS at QP2-CR. The normal operations on the wells should be done through the choke valves according to the production demand. See chapter 11 for the Wellhead Operations.

4.4. NORMAL SHUTDOWN

The Hydraulic Pumps can be stopped either form the WHCP stop push button if the remote / local switch is in local from the HMI or from the Central Control Room if the remote / local switch is in remote.

4.5. EMERGENCY SHUTDOWN

There is an Emergency .push button on the WHCP front face for each Hydraulic Pump P-0042 A/B, P-0043 A/B and P-0045.
The Auxiliary Pump P-0045 will also trip by ESD-1
The VHP Pumps P-0042 A/B will trip by ESD-3.5
The HP Pumps P-0043 A/B will trip by ESD-3.6
