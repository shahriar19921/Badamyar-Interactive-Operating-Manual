CHAPTER 5 SAFETY & CRITICAL SYSTEM – FIREFIGHTING SYSTEM

SECTION 1 : INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

1.1.1 Purpose

  • The objectives of the firefighting systems are to:
  • control fires and limit escalation,
  • reduce the effects of a fire to allow personnel to evacuate,
  • extinguish the fire where it is considered safe to do so,
  • limit damages to structures and equipment.

1.1.2 System Criticality

As it preserves the safety of the personnel and the integrity of the equipment, the firefighting system is one of the most critical systems of the platform.

1.1.3 Key components

WP4 firefighting systems are composed of the following main pieces of equipment:

  • Dry chemical extinguishers
  • CO2 extinguishers
  • Hydrants
  • Vent Snuffing system
  • Fi Fi Boat

SECTION 2 : SECTION 2 SYSTEM / EQUIPMENT DETAILED DESCRIPTION

2.1 ACTIVE FIRE PROTECTION

2.1.1 Fixed Fire Water system

Fixed firewater systems on WP4 are restricted to the provision of a fixed dry riser type system extending from the surfer landing up to the upper deck. The dry riser will enable the drilling rig to deliver water to the system during drilling or workover periods. One ring-main is formed underneath main deck to distribute the firewater to the hydrants. Hydrants provide the ability to fight fire of limited extent.
During drilling and workover the standby vessel will provide fire fighting means for more extensive fires.
The fixed dry riser system is made of copper-nickel and designed to enable the delivery of water on each deck at opposite locations. The flow rate in the riser is limited to 120 m3 /h as per GS EP SAF 311.
Two external connections are provided for firewater supply.

  • The connection from drilling rig at weather deck
  • The connection at surfer deck from external supply

The required pressure for the Firewater pumps from the drilling rig or the Supply boat has been calculated as follows:
  • From Drilling Rig at Weather Deck = 7.14 barg
  • From Supply Boat at Surfer Landing = 9.46 barg

2.1.2 Portable Extinguishers

Extinguishers of two main types are used:

  • Extinguishers on wheels, weight of 70 kg, 50 kg and 20 kg (units are large wheeled type and suitable for grated decks)
  • Portable extinguishers of 5 or 9 kg.
Two main types of hand held portable extinguishers shall be used;

  • Dry chemical for fire class A, B, C;
  • CO2 for fire class B, C.

One wheeled dry chemical unit (70 kg) is located on each of the main deck, the mezzanine deck and the cellar deck.

Portable extinguishers are located in readily accessible locations on WP4, for example, close to transformers and on escape ways.

Fire extinguishers selected for each location must be based upon the fire hazard class present in that area.

Extinguishers are of certified type designed to resist UV and saline environment.

2.1.3 Vent Snuffing System

The WP4 cold vent is fitted with a 2x45 kg carbon dioxide snuffing system with 100% back up:
Main or Reserve CO2 Bottle are equipped with solenoid valve, which can be selected by HS-50032.

The solenoid valves function are based on duty/standby principle. If after time delay the selected solenoid valve activation does not initiate the alarm PAH-50034 indicating the release of the CO2 bottle then Reserve valve will opened.

The release of the CO2 will be known in the Central Control Room by the PAH-50034 and on site by the audible sound XA-50035.

2.1.4 Fi Fi Boat

The standby vessel will be in attendance and will provide the main fire fighting capability during periods of drilling and well intervention.

It is noted that the current standby boat is equipped with 1 fire pump with 1,200 m3/hr capacity and 2 fire monitors with 600 m3/hr capacity whist the firewater demand on WP4, as per the firewater demand calculation, is 120 m3/hr.

Hence the requirement at WP4 is much lower than the capacity available from the standby vessel.

2.1.5 Helideck Fire Fighting

The helideck on the Upper deck is steel plated and is provided with wheeled dry chemical extinguishers and CO2 extinguishers at weather deck (EL.+ 27100).
A Helicopter rescue kit cabinet is also located to both the North and South sides of the weather deck.

2.2 PASSIVE FIRE PROTECTION (PFP)

Passive fire protection is provided to protect vulnerable hydrocarbon inventories, safety systems and ESVs that could be impacted by heat radiation from jet and sea surface pool fires.

Regarding WP4 which is a Normally Unattended Installation (NUI) platform, WP4 is not specifically intended to be protected by PFP. According to the results from FERA there are no recommendations for PFP application at WP4 as the risks are deemed acceptable.
