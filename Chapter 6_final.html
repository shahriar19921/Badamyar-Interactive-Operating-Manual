CHAPTER 6 SAFETY & CRITICAL SYSTEM – INTEGRATED CONTROL & SAFETY SYSTEM

SECTION 1 : INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

1.1.1 Purpose

The Integrated Control and Safety System (ICSS) is a distributed control and safety system designed to reduce, to as low as reasonably practicable, the potential hazards to personnel and the environment. The system comprises:

  • Process Control System (PCS), which provides routine operator control, monitoring, alarming, reporting and short-term data archiving functions of all Yadana facilities,
  • Plant Safety System (PSS), which protects the facility from process upsets, control system failures and equipment malfunctions. PSS comprises:

    •         - Emergency Shutdown (ESD) System: there are three levels of shutdown in WP4, ESD-1, ESD-2 and ESD-3, performed by the ESD which provide protection for the whole Installation by shutdown, depending upon the seriousness of the hazard detected,
               - Fire and Gas System (FGS) for fire detection and gas detection, and associated alarming and protection functions.

1.1.2 System Criticality

The ICSS criticality is paramount to the safe operation of the BADAMAYAR facility. Without ICSS, the whole equipment and component of YADANA field cannot be functioning and should plant equipment or any component malfunction, fail or develop a fault, or a combination of these events, then the field will be shutdown.
Any fault or unsafe condition which develops whether genuine or spurious will result in automatic shutdown to revert the YADANA field into a safe condition.

SECTION 2 : SECTION 2 SYSTEM / EQUIPMENT DETAILED DESCRIPTION

2.1 PROCESS CONTROL SYSTEM

New PCS equipment supplied for LCP-Badamyar project (both WP4 and LCP) are based on Yokogawa Centum-VP. Controller network shall be based on Vnet/IP. Network extensions shall be based on fibre optics.

The existing graphic display hierarchy will be completed with the additional WP4 and LCP related graphic displays.

The existing application programme principles, the typicals, (e.g. colour coding, alarm handling, soft targets, block logic typical, etc…) is re-used, but adapted for CS 3000 R5 (VP) software, and further expanded as required.

PDMS system (PI) shall be modified to take into account the additional data relevant to the new WP4 and LCP.

If required, WP-4 well control logics shall be modified later to be in line with RFM Y_375 requirements, which has been very recently implemented in YADANA and applied to WP1/WP2/WP3 well heads and choke valves.

2.2 PLANT SAFETY SYSTEM

PSS process the Emergency Shutdown and the Fire and Gas logics.
New PSS equipment for WP4 is SIL 3 Trident PLC from Invensys. with TCM4351B communication cards. Four cards are required, two for the serial communication with PCS and two for the peer-to-peer communication with QP PSS.

The PLC programs for ESD and F&G system are written in such a way that these two functions are independent of each other. The inputs and outputs cards dedicated to the Emergency Shutdown are segregated from the inputs and outputs cards dedicated to the Fire and Gas including the rack segregation.

PSS has dual redundant communication link with PCS data highway. This link allows for transmission of data for display and logging on PCS. It shall also allow:

  • Manual start-up overrides from PCS Operator Stations,
  • Reset and re-start functions at PCS Operator Stations.
New PSS is connected to the existing peer to peer network used to transfer information from all the Fire and Gas systems to the existing QP2 Fire and Gas marshalling cabinets i.e. F&G-6-MC1 and F&G-6-MC2. The peer to peer network is used to collect all the information required for the F&G Matrix Panel.
New PSS is connected to the existing maintenance network that is use for:
  • Inhibit/isolate I/Os from the maintenance station (through an existing PC connected to the network and loaded with a dedicated Intouch application),
  • Force outputs from the maintenance station (through an existing PC connected to the network and loaded with a dedicated Intouch application),
  • Sequence of Event recording (through an existing PC connected to the network with a dedicated TRICONEX SOE application),
New PSS alarms are recorded on the existing SOE computer.
New network extension (Peer-to-Peer and maintenance networks) is interconnected to the existing PSS network at PP Instrument Technical Room.
