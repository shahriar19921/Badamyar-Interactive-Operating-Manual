1.1. INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

1.1.1 Purpose

The Wellhead Control Panel (WHCP) is housing the Hydraulic Power Unit 1 U-0040 which is in charge of controlling the opening and closing of the following valves on each well:

  • Downhole Safety Valves(DHSV)
  • Upper Master Valve (UMV)
  • Wing Valves (WV)

Hide image

Hide image

Hide image

In addition of the hydraulic power to control the wellhead valves, the hydraulic Power Unit 1 is feeding the ESD-1 and ESD-2 Headers, part of the control logic of the WP4 platform.

DHSV is operated by the VHP oil pressure of 350 barg. UMV and WV are operated by HP oil pressure of 230 barg. Control logic is by LP pressure of 5.5 barg.

The valve opening sequence is manual from WHCP front face of the drawer, well by well, valve by valve, however it is interlocked hydraulically by WHCP to impose the opening order.

The opening sequence is:

  • DHSV - 1st
  • UMV - 2nd
  • WV - 3rd

Hide image

Hide image

Hide image

However, facility on each well control module allows the out of sequence opening of the UMV while the WV and DHSV remain closed. This is in order to check the passing rate of the DHSV. The out of sequence switch is lockable and front of panel mounted. It shall only be operable after a wellhead has been fully shutdown. Any ESD function will override the sequence and shut the master valve.

The valve closing sequence can be activated from the platform control system or locally from WHCP. If operated locally it will be a valve by valve operation. The following closing sequence shall be hydraulically performed and shall be never altered.
  • WV - 1st
  • UMV - 2nd
  • DHSV - 3rd
Adjustable timers (0-60 sec) is provided in the module logic enabling delays between valve operations for closing between WV and UMV and another between closing UMV and DHSV. The response time for full shutdown shall not take more than 30 sec.

ESD1 of WP4 closes the DHSV, UMV and WV of all wells, ESD2 closes the UMV and WV of all wells, and ESD3.1X closes the UMV and WV of the respective well.

Action on wellhead platform valves is via solenoid valves (de-energized to trip) located inside the WHCP cabinet ( XY-50065 for ESD-1,XY-50067 for ESD-2, and XY-5X018 for ESD3.1X).

In addition, local interlocking between DHSV and UMV’s shall also be provided. This shall close the UMV, or prevent the UMV from opening, in case of loss of pressure in the DHSV control line. It is achieved by mean of a hydraulic pressure pilot, operated by the hydraulic pressure to the DHSV, installed in series with the solenoid pilot valve on each UMV control line.

1.1.2 System Criticality

The Wellhead Control Panel and its Hydraulic Power Unit is essential for the well’s production, the protection of personnel and equipment on the WP4 wells. Without this system the function of the platform would be inoperable.

SECTION 2 SYSTEM / EQUIPMENT DETAILED DESCRIPTION

2.1. EQUIPMENT DESCRIPTION

Hydraulic Pump P-0042A/B,(VHP)

Hide image

The HPU1 (U-0040) is equipped with two sets of hydraulic pumps (radial piston type) in duty/standby mode (2 x 100%) for VHP circuit. The hydraulic pumps A and B are equipped with electric motors.

The local control for the electric pumps is performed by two start/stop pushbuttons. One emergency stop pushbutton is provided for each pump. The local controls are installed on the front face of the WHCP. Operator from QP2-CR can control the pumps from DCS when the local/remote selector is in remote selection. The local/remote, duty/standby selector is selectable by operator from the DCS HMI at WP4 Instrument Shelter, and from QP2-CR DCS.

In remote, the pump start/stop can be performed in manual by operator from the HMI and DCS or in auto mode, by low and high pressure at the pump discharge.

Another ESD pressure transmitter at the pump discharge creates an ESD action in the event of alarm high high and low-low header pressure PT-50055.

The pumps are connected to the hydraulic tank, which is provided with a non-return foot valve and a suction filter provided between the tank and pump. The discharge line is provided with a discharge header and non-return valve.

In remote, the pump start/stop can be performed in manual by operator from the HMI and DCS or in auto mode, by low and high pressure at the pump discharge.

The pumps are connected to the hydraulic tank, which is provided with a non-return foot valve and a suction filter provided between the tank and pump. The discharge line is provided with a discharge header and non-return valve.

2.1.3 LP Header

The LP Header is pressurized from the HP Header after reducing the pressure through 1 set of self-regulating pressure valves PCV-50066 A/B (one on line and the other in stand-by) which reduced the pressure to 5.5 barg. The LP header is connected to the ESD-1 and ESD-2 headers.

Hide image

2.1.4 DHSV Return Tank(T-0043)

Hide image

A dedicated return tank within the panel is provided for the hydraulic oil return from the DHSV. The oil return tank is sufficiently sized to contain 115% of the total volume of all DHSV actuators.

The tank comprises a settlement chamber, accepting the fluid return line, with a pipe from this chamber to the reserve tank T-0042. This pipe is filled by overflow from the settlement chamber. The flow of hydraulic fluid through this pipe to the reserve tank T-0042 is routed through a filter by using the Auxiliary Pump P-0045.

The tank is equipped with a flame arrestor breather IP 45.

The combined tank is sized to contain 150% of the complete cold fill of the hydraulic system, including all valves actuators. The tank comprises a settlement chamber, accepting the fluid return lines.

Filling of the tank is possible from outside of the panel. The filling system is provided with a captive plug and a filter.

2.1.6 Accumulators

A set of 2 x 20 L accumulators is provided to maintain the pressure of the VHP header in case of shut-down of one of the hydraulic pump the time that the stand-by one is running properly. The same system is provided for the HP header (3 x 37 L accumulators) as well as for the LP Header (2 x 5 L accumulators. They are of hydraulic bladder type with nitrogen charging medium.

Facilities are provided to safely check and recharge the nitrogen pre-charge (isolating valves and pressure gauges). A pre-charge kit is provided with the HPU. The accumulators are fitted with double block and bleed valves on the oil side.

SECTION 4 OPERATING MODES

4.1. EQUIPMENT OPERATING MODES

There are two operating mode for the Hydraulic Pumps P-0042, P-0043 and P-0045:

  • Local from the WHCP front side
  • Remote from the HMI of the Well Control Module or from the PCS in QP2 Control Room.
The local control for the electric pumps is performed by:
  • Two start/stop pushbuttons.
  • One emergency stop pushbutton
The remote control from QP2- CR allows the following selection:
  • Local / remote
  • Duty / Stand-by
The DHSV, UMV and WV valves opening is manual from WHCP front face of the drawer, well by well, valve by valve the following opening sequence:
  • DHSV - 1st
  • UMV - 2nd
  • WV - 3rd
The valve closing sequence is either on command from the platform control system or locally from WHCP. If operated locally that is valve by valve local operate, following closing sequence automatically hydraulically performed.
  • WV - 1st
  • UMV - 2nd
  • DHSV - 3rd
Action on wellhead platform valves from QP2 is via solenoid valves (de-energized to trip) located inside the WHCP cabinet (XY-50065 for ESD-1, XY-50066 for ESD-2, and XY-5X018 for ESD3.1X).

4.2. NORMAL START-UP

The normal start-up of the Wellhead Control Panel will be done locally with all pumps in “local position” and the wells valves opened locally from the WHCP.

4.3. NORMAL OPERATIONS

The normal operations of the Hydraulic Pumps after start-up should be from the DCS at QP2-CR. The normal operations on the wells should be done through the choke valves according to the production demand. See chapter 11 for the Wellhead Operations.

4.4. NORMAL SHUTDOWN

The Hydraulic Pumps can be stopped either form the WHCP stop push button if the remote / local switch is in local from the HMI or from the Central Control Room if the remote / local switch is in remote.

4.5. EMERGENCY SHUTDOWN

There is an Emergency .push button on the WHCP front face for each Hydraulic Pump P-0042 A/B, P-0043 A/B and P-0045.
The Auxiliary Pump P-0045 will also trip by ESD-1
The VHP Pumps P-0042 A/B will trip by ESD-3.5
The HP Pumps P-0043 A/B will trip by ESD-3.6
