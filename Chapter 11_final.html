SECTION 1 INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

        1.1.1 Purpose

The first platforms were installed in 1998 in a water depth of 45 m and the MCP platform was installed in 2007. The objective of the MCP was to maintain gas sales production while wellhead flowing pressure decreases.

Phase 4 of Yadana Development and therefore the installation of WP4 and LCP Platforms includes the development of a low pressure reservoir, Badamyar as well as the main reservoir Yadana and Sein in low pressure. The development is to maintain the plateau of production of Yadana.

WP4 is initially in operation with 4 gas producing wells (drilled in 2016, operated from 2017), with the proposed installation of 2 additional wells in 2019 (in operation in 2020).

The wellheads integrate the facilities to control the well production while providing the maximum possible safety devices to close the wells in case of abnormal situation.


        1.1.2 System Criticality

This system is critical for safe operations and business continuity of the YADANA complex.

DHSV is operated by the VHP oil pressure of 350 barg. UMV and WV are operated by HP oil pressure of 230 barg. Control logic is by LP pressure of 5.5 barg.

All actuators are fail safe, spring return type: If hydraulic pressure is applied, valve shall be opened.

The valve opening sequence is manual from WHCP front face of the drawer, well by well, valve by valve, and it is interlocked hydraulically by WHCP to impose the opening order.

The opening sequence is:
      •   DHSV - 1st
      •   UMV - 2nd
      •   WV - 3rd

However, facility on each well control module allows the out of sequence opening of the UMV while the WV and DHSV remain closed. This is in order to leak test the DHSV. The out of sequence switch is lockable and front of panel mounted. It shall only be operable after a wellhead has been fully shutdown. Any ESD function will override the sequence and shut the master valve.

The valve closing sequence can be activated from the platform control system or locally from WHCP. If operated locally it will be a valve by valve operation. The following closing sequence shall be hydraulically performed and shall be never altered.
      •   WV - 1st
      •   UMV - 2nd
      •   DHSV - 3rd

Adjustable timers (0-60 sec) is provided in the module logic enabling delays between valve operations for closing between WV and UMV and another between closing UMV and DHSV. The response time for full shutdown shall not take more than 30 sec.

In addition, local interlocking between DHSV and UMV’s is also provided. This shall close the UMV, or prevent the UMV from opening, in case of loss of pressure in the DHSV control line. It is achieved by mean of a hydraulic pressure pilot, operated by the hydraulic pressure to the DHSV, installed in series with the solenoid pilot valve on each UMV control line. Wing valve remote opening is possible when wing valve is closed voluntarily by the PCS.

The Choke Valves for each Well are operated by the MP Hydraulic Oil as power medium from the Hydraulic Panel Unit No. 2 (U-0044). The pressure of the MP header to the choke valve actuators is maintained between 115 barg (Hydraulic Pumps P-0044 A/B stop) to 85 barg (Hydraulic Pumps P-0044 A/B start.

3.1. CONTROL DESCRIPTION


        3.1.1 Wellhead Control from LCP & WP4 Control logic

See Drawing “Plot Plan Mezzanine Deck” MM-YAD-91-HHIE-160002 for the location of the Wellheads.

The WP4 Control Logic, interfacing with the LCP Control Logic has the following two main functions:

      •     Flowline Choke valve Control

      •     Process control (closure) of Wing Valves. Wing valve and Choke Valve automatic operation is controlled by LCP and WP4 Control Logic, related to the Pressure Control of LCP Inlet Manifold PICA-80012

and WP4 outlet pressure PICA-50004

If the LCP Inlet Manifold Pressure exceeds the first threshold P1 (PICAH-80012)

If the LCP Inlet Manifold Pressure exceeds the second threshold P2 PICAHH-80012 at 90/46/18 barg for MP/LP/LLP operating mode respectively), wing valves selected by operator beforehand and the associated choke valves are closed.

If the LCP Inlet Manifold Pressure exceeds the third threshold P3 (PAHH-80004) at 94/49/20 barg for MP/LP/LLP operating mode respectively) or WP4 outlet pressure PICA-50004 exceeds high-high threshold (hold), all WP4 wing valves and the associated choke valves are closed.

The preselected position value shall be less than current choke valve position.


        3.1.2 Wells Pressure Control

Note: In the following paragraph the letter “x” is replacing the well number from 1 to 4

A pressure transmitter PIT-5x008 located on the flowline between the Wing Valve and the corresponding Choke Valve send its signal to PSS to activates the low low pressure alarm PALL-5x008   set at 52 barg / 25 barg / 10 barg respectively in MP / LP / LLP operating mode. The reaching of this threshold will initiate an ESD-3.1.x (where x is the well No.).

PALL-5x008 Hide image

The initiation of the ESD-3.1x will have the following consequences:

      •     Close XY-5x018

      •     solenoid valve on ESD-3 hydraulic supply header

               -     Close WV-5x004

               -     Close UMV-5x003 after time delay

Another pressure transmitter PIT-5x007 located as well on the flowline between the Wing Valve and the corresponding Choke Valve send its signal to PCS and activates the low pressure alarm PIAL-5x007 set at 70 barg / 34 barg / 18 barg respectively in MP / LP / LLP operating mode and the high pressure alarm PIAH-5x007 set at 96 barg / 76 barg / 47 barg respectively in MP / LP/ LLP operating mode.

Downstream of the choke valves, the pressure transmitter PIT-5x013 is provided. It sends its signal to PCS activating a low pressure alarm PIAL-5x013 set at 57 barg / 28 barg / 14 barg in MP / LP / LLP operating mode, respectively. As well a high pressure alarm PIAH-5x013 activates at 74 barg / 50 barg / 24 barg in MP / LP / LLP operating mode, respectively. PIT-5x013 is also used to input the pressure value in the correction calculator of the FIAH-5x019.

Finally the last pressure safety device is by the pressure transmitter PIT-5x014 located upstream of the production manifold. This transmitter sends its signal to PSS and activates a low low pressure alarm  PALL-5x014 at 52 barg / 25 barg / 10 barg in MP / LP / LLP operating mode, respectively. A high high pressure alarm  PAHH-5x014 activates at 76 barg / 52 barg / 25 barg in MP / LP / LLP operating mode, respectively. The activation of the   PALL-5x014 or  PAHH-5x014 initiates an ESD-3.1x.

        3.1.3 Wells Temperature Control

In order to anticipate the risk of hydrates formation, a temperature transmitter TIT-5x006 has been installed upstream of the choke valve and near the methanol injection. A high temperature alarm TIAH-5x006 is activated at 42 °C.

Another temperature transmitter TIT-5x015 is also used to input the temperature value in the correction calculator of the FIAH-5x019. A low temperature alarm TIAL-5x015 is activated at 20 °C.

Some other temperature instrument installed:

      •     TG-5x012, upstream of choke valve, near the methanol injection point.

      •     TG-5x017, on the flowline upstream of the vent line. (venting monitoring).


        3.1.4 Flowline flow control

The well flow is monitored by the flow transmitter FIT-5x019, located on the flowline to Production Manifold. It gives signal to FIA-5x019 giving an instant flow indication in MMSCFD and to a flow totalizer FQI-5x019. A high flow alarm FIAH-5x019 activates at 39 MMSCFD.


        3.1.5 Sand & Erosion Monitoring Panel

The Sand and Erosion Monitoring Panel will provides the following indications to the PCS:

      •     Erosion Indication (XI-5x025)

      •     Erosion Alarm High (XAH-5x025)

      •     Sand Rate Indication (XI-5x026)

      •     Sand Rate Alarm High (XAH-5x0260)


For this purpose 2 sensors are installed:

      •     XT-5x026, which is an acoustic sand detector (non-intrusive) installed downstream of choke valve.

      •     XE-5x025, which is a non-intrusive clamp-on ultrasonic type erosion monitoring, installed downstream of the choke valve on a elbow.

SECTION 4 : OPERATING MODES


4.1. OPERATING MODES

There are essentially 3 sub-phases of operation of LCP, medium pressure (MP), low pressure (LP), and Low Low Pressure (LLP).

      •     Initially, the pressure at the FWKOD’s on MCP platform is such that the two MP Compressors on MCP can provide the compression power                 (operating as 2 x 50% compressors in parallel) to achieve the export gas pressure required (105.5 barg). This is the MP phase in which the                 LP Compressors will be bypassed and flow will go directly to the MP Compressors.

      •     The pressure will decline further and the LP Compressors (2 x 50% machines operating in parallel) will be required to boost pressure to the MP             Compressor suction (providing a pressure range of 58.2 to 68.2 barg) at the outlet of the LP Compressor Discharge Air Coolers. This is the LP              phase.

      •     Later, pressure and fslow will decline which requires further booster compression. This is the LLP phase and will be achieved by running the      two LP compressors in series. Train A compressor will be modified, restaged, and will boost pressure to a range of 29.9 to 30.2 barg for feed to the             LP Compressor (Train B) which in turn will boost the pressure to 56.4 barg for feed to the MP Compressors. In this mode of operation, the LP            compressors will be run as 100% machines in series. The MP Compressors will become 100% machines operating in duty/standby mode.

        





4.2. NORMAL START-UP

Initially the WP4 pressure and other well platform pressures will allow the central complex to operate in MP Mode and the LP Compressor will be by-passed after commissioning. The following system have to be ready before starting any wellhead

      •     All XVs on the flow lines to the Production and Test Manifolds should be closed.

      •     All drain lines should be locked closed and positively isolated from the closed drains.

      •     The Wet Gas meter will be isolated.

      •     The Pig Launcher will be closed in and isolated and vented to atmospheric pressure.

      •     Methanol should be available for starting up of the wells in case any water has been left in the lines and in case of low temperature to guard             against hydrate formation. The well Christmas trees are provided with methanol injection points. This should be hooked up to a methanol tote                tank ready for injection if required.

      •     The Corrosion Inhibitor Tank should be full and ready for start-up.

      •     The hydraulic oil reservoirs in the HPU 1and 2 should be full.

      •     Electric Power should be available on the platform.

      •     ICSS operational

      •     F&G detections in service,

      •     Firefighting ready

      •     Closed drain and Vent system ready

      •     Open Drain ready

        





4.3. NORMAL OPERATIONS

The normal operations on the wellhead and at WP4 generally will mainly consist to control and adjust from the Central Control Room the opening of the choke valves according to the gas demand.

        





4.4. NORMAL SHUTDOWN

For a normal shutdown the Operator will activate the XY-5x018 (x being the well to shutdown). This will close the WV first then the UMV with time delay.
