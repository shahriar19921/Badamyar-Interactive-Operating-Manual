CHAPTER 14 EXTRACTION WELLS & FLOWLINES – PIG LAUNCHER

SECTION 1 INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

1.1.1 Purpose

The Pig Launcher is provided to avoid accumulation of deposits on the wall of the pipe, contributing to increase the DP of the sea line between WP4 and LCP.

1.1.2 System Criticality

This system is not critical to continue producing gas for some limited period of time.

1.1.3 Key components

The key components of the Pig Launcher are the following:

  • 14” ball valve by-pass of the Pig Launcher
  • 16” ball valve downstream of the Barred Tee to WP4 sealine
  • 2 x 8” ball valves on the kicker line of the Pig Launcher
  • 2 x 16” ball valves outside of the Pig Launcher upstream of the Barred Tee
  • Pig Signalers ZS-50011 and ZS-50015

SECTION 2 SYSTEM / EQUIPMENT DETAILED DESCRIPTION

2.1. SYSTEM DESCRIPTION

See Drawing
“Wellhead Platform – WP4-Pig Launcher” - MM-YAD-91-HHIE-150107 - Rev 06

Hide image

The Pig Launcher (PL-0040) is a horizontal vessel with a total length of 4494 mm. The internal diameter of the major barrel is 458 mm while that of the minor barrel is 377.8 mm. The vessel is made of carbon steel with 3 mm corrosion allowance, and is designed for 116.5 barg and 64/-29 deg °C.

The Pig Launcher should be drain and left under slight positive pressure of Nitrogen after each launching operation. To protect the vessel against overpressure, one pressure relief valve for fire case is provided. Two different ranges of pressure gauge (High and low range) are provided on the Pig Launcher. The door of the Pig Launcher is of quick-opening type and equipped with a mechanical safety device to only allow door opening after barrel depressurization.

Pig signallers (ZS-50011/50015) are installed upstream and downstream of the barred tee to provide indication of pig movement both locally and in the QP2 control room. There are no interlock system provided for the Pig Launcher valving / door opening operations, therefore the Operating Procedure for Pigging Operation should be strictly followed.

3.1. CONTROL DESCRIPTION

3.1.1 Production Header Pressure Control

Upstream of the barred tee, the pressure is monitored by PICA-50004 acting as well in the WP4 Control Logic. Alarms are provided to alert the operator about system upset. A low pressure alarm PICAL-50004 is set at 57 barg / 28 barg / 14 barg for MP / LP / LLP Operating Mode respectively. A high pressure alarm PICAH-50004 is set at 74 barg / 50 barg / 24 barg for MP / LP / LLP Operating Mode respectively.
The low low pressure trip ( PALL-50007A/B/C) with 2oo3 voting set at 52 / 25 / 10 barg (MP / LP / LLP) is provided upstream of the Pipeline. PALL-50007A/B/C will cause WP4 ESD 1.02 . This ESD level will initiate the following action;

5.2.3 Hazardous sources

Hazard Source Hazardous Event Effect Control
Fluid under pressure Throughout system Uncontrolled release of energy Potential for personnel injury Routine inspections
gaseous Hydrocarbons Throughout system potential for gas release and fire/explosion Potential for personnel injury Fire and Gas detection
When Pig Launcher is in operation (PSV Fire Case) and there is fire on it, hydrocarbon can be emitted through a relief valve and then through the vent tip. Even though the possibility is low, crane operation is not permitted when Pig Launcher is in operation.

5.2.4 Nitrogen

Caution should be taken while using nitrogen to inert the Pig Launcher. Depressurization under nitrogen should be done exclusively through vent valves connected to the Vent Header or to the Save Location venting system of the Pig Launcher PL-0040
