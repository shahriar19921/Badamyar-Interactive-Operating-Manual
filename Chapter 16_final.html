CHAPTER 16 UTILITIES – CLOSED DRAIN AND VENT SYSTEM

SECTION 1 INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

1.1.1 Purpose

Any facility containing hazardous liquids which must be drained for operational reasons and cannot be drained directly to atmosphere without undue risk to personnel, environment or assets from release of flammable or toxic vapours shall be connected to a closed drain system.

The closed drain system connects drains used for maintenance purposes, i.e. to drain a facility after it has been isolated and depressurised (the residual pressure before drainage shall not exceed 3.5 barg).

As for the Vent system the purpose is that considering higher pressure design than WHSIP (97barg) and no implementation of emergency depressurization, the only venting cases are manual depressurization, maintenance depressurization, and offloading. Vent Header is connected to the Closed Drain Drum and Vent Knock-out Drum

1.1.2 System Criticality

The availability of the closed drains system is not directly essential to maintain production until there is no maintenance operations or offloading of wells.

1.1.3 Key components

The system comprises the following main pieces of equipment:

  • Closed drain headers
  • PSVs discharge P-0040A/B header
  • Vent header
  • Offloading line
Each of one has its dedicated nozzle on closed drain drum with dip pipe for the 2 first and deflectors for others.

The header has rodding out connection and utility connection for purging and flushing.

The CD drum D-0040 for WP4 also functions as a Vent Knock Out drum.

Liquids are pumped back into the production manifold through 2x100% reciprocating pumps P-0040A/B .

2.1.2 Closed Drain Drum and Vent Knock-out Drum D-0040

The purpose of Closed Drain Drum D-0040 is to collect maintenance drains from equipment and lines. The drum is also designed as a vent knock out drum.

The Closed Drain Drum D-0040 is made of carbon steel with 3 mm CA and provided with internal coating and sacrificial anodes. The drum dimension is 6,000 mm T/T with an inside diameter of 2,000 mm. The vessel size is based on the working volume of 10 m3 between LAL and LAH to accommodate the maximum maintenance draining as well as for offloading operation.

The drum is a horizontal two-phase separator. Its design pressure and temperature are 15 barg and 64°C max / -29°C min. The specified design pressure is to satisfy the requirement for the Closed Drain Drum D-0040 to withstand internal explosion.

Considering higher pressure design than the Wellhead Shut-in Pressure (WHSIP) = 97barg) and no implementation of emergency depressurization, the only venting cases are manual depressurization, maintenance depressurization, and offloading.

The design is such as to limit the venting and/or flaring to 5 MMSCFD. See Safety Concept WP4 No. MM-YAD-90-HHIE-110100 on the reasons why there is no EDP (derogation 2013-DEROG-HSE-044).

Offloading

Initially on WP4, reservoir pressure will be high and no water will be produced. As the wells age, the reservoir pressure decreases and water is produced. Eventually water in the well tubing will cause a decrease in production flow of gas until a point is reached when the well is not producing a viable gas flow. Eventually the flow may stop altogether as the reservoir pressure is not high enough to overcome the column of water in the well tubing and flow to the pipeline at its operating pressure. At this point it becomes necessary to clear water from the well tubing by flowing to a lower pressure, namely to the Closed Drain/Vent Knockout Drum which operates at atmospheric pressure. This is called “Offloading”. It is predicted that the WP4 platform will produce water in the future and at some stage wells may require offloading. The platform is designed to facilitate offloading.

A separate inlet nozzle (12”) is provided on the Closed Drain Drum and Vent KO Drum D-0040 for the offloading line.

A vent tip and an ignition panel are required for the offloading operation at the end of field life. The ignition system is of High Energy Ignition type per GS EP SAF 262. The vent tip is permanently installed on WP4, however the ignition system (U-0043) and related items are installed when the requirement for well offloading is confirmed.

A nitrogen bottles skid U-0045 is provided for purging the cold vent before and after use of ignition system. A separate propane bottles skid U-0046 is provided for sweeping the vent header during well offloading operation. Both of these packages (U-0045 and U-0046) will be installed only when the need of offloading is confirmed

A permanently installed CO2 snuffing system U-0042 is provided to guard against accidental vent ignition during normal operation. This system will be deactivated during offloading operation by inhibit of the temperature detection XA-50075 / XS-50075

Radiations

Since thermal radiation level at Crane cabin exceeds the emergency flaring criteria of 4.7 kW/m2 and the continuous flaring criteria of 3.2 kW/m2, crane operation is not permitted when vent tip is ignited or when there is a possibility of hydrocarbon emission through vent tip. These situations are during offloading and manual depressurization.

If Pig Launcher is in operation (PSV Fire Case) and there is fire on it, hydrocarbon can be emitted through a relief valve and then through the vent tip. Even though the possibility is low, crane operation is not permitted when Pig Launcher is in operation.

Thermal radiation levels of Propane Bottle Skid, CO2 Snuffing Package, Flame Ignition Package, N2 Bottle Skid exceed continuous flaring criteria of 3.2 kW/m2. In order to meet these criteria, the current vent boom length would have to be increased by 8 m. However, the current vent boom length of 15.4 m can be retained provided that:

  • Heat shields for Propane Bottle Skid and N2 Bottle Skid, Flame Ignition Package are to be provided by vendor to protect the bottles from thermal radiation and high equilibrium temperature.
For CO2 Snuffing Package, the CO2 bottles are inside the cabinet and they are not directly exposed to thermal radiation.

Hearing protections shall be enforced at WP4 Platform during offloading operation. PAGA audible level and alarm shall be reviewed to ensure that its clarity will not be hindered by the hearing protection.

For more details refer to the “Vent Radiation Report for WP4” - MM-YAD-90-HHIE-131001 rev 03.

2.1.3 Closed Drain Pumps P-0040 A/B

Hide image

The Closed Drain Pumps (P-0040A/B) are of reciprocating type with a design capacity of 10 m3/hr each.

Design pressure and temperature are 116.5 barg and 64°C, respectively. The Closed Drain Pumps take suction from the Closed Drain Drum D-0040 and discharge to export line.

The Closed Drain Pumps (P-0040A/B) are equipped with suction strainer, pulsation dampeners at the suction and discharge of the pump, PSV (PSV-50305 for pump A / PSV-50205 for pump B) set at 115,5 barg in case of blocked discharge.

The Closed Drain Pumps P-0040A/B are in duty-standby configuration. The pumps can be operated in local or remote. The local/remote and duty/standby selectors are selectable by operator from HMI.

Stand by pump starts when the duty pumps stops for fault. During maintenance activities pumps are set in manual local mode.

2.1.4 Closed Drain Pumps P-0040 A/B and Well Offloading

Well offloading will be performed through the Closed Drain Drum and Vent KO Drum D-0040. It is designed to allow recovery of 35 m3 from the well tubing (based on the water liquid column of the production tubing, assuming 5½’’ tubing diameter), considering closed drain pumps (2x100%) will re-send the liquid (essentially water) recovered to the production at a rate of 10 m3/h. Considering a minimum offloading duration of 2.5 hrs, the liquid holding volume of the drum is then required to be 10 m3.

The pumps installed for closed drain system are reciprocating pumps. To further minimize offloading time, the option of running both closed drain pumps simultaneously during offloading has been taken into consideration.

With two pumps running and 10 m3 liquid holding volume, an offloading rate of 28 m3/hr is possible with a theoretical minimum offloading time assuming slug flow of liquid up the riser, of 1.25 hours. The actual offloading time will most likely be longer due to the bubble flow regime in the tubing.

3.1.4 CO2 Snuffing Package Control

A CO2 snuffing system is provided to guard against accidental vent ignition during maintenance activities. The temperature sensor TE-50075 sends its signal to a Flame Indication XA-50075. Upon receiving a signal above 176°C, XA-50075 will activate the CO2 Snuffing system by energizing Main or Reserve CO2 Bottle solenoid valve, depending on which set has been selected by the duty / stand-by selector HS-50032.

If after time delay since the selected solenoid valve activates, the pressure switch PS-50034 does not initiate indicating the release of the CO2 bottle then Reserve valve will opened.

The release of the CO2 will be known in the Central Control Room by the PAH-50034 and on site by the audible sound XA-50035.

When the temperature controlled by TE-50075 will return back to normal, the solenoid valve will deactivate.

CO2 snuffing package shall be inhibited during well offloading

3.1.5 Equipment Controlled only for Offloading Operation

Nitrogen Package U-0045
The nitrogen package U-0045 will be installed when the need of offloading is confirmed.

The PCV-50048 set at 0.5 barg will regulate the nitrogen pressure to the Vent Header. A pressure safety valve PSV-50047 set at 15 barg will protect the pipe. The FI-50021 will give locally the nitrogen flow to be adjusted by the means of the upstream needle valve.

Propane Package U-0046

The propane package is provided to purge the Vent Header during the offloading operation. It will be installed when the need of offloading is confirmed

The PCV-50045 set at 0.5 barg will regulate the propane pressure to the Vent Header. The flow of propane will be adjusted with the needle valve and the FI-50021 will give the local flow indication.

Vent Ignition Package U-0043

Will be installed when the need of offloading is confirmed. The Vent tip consists in one type of ignition system: Automatic / Manual, High Energy Igniter (HEI).

The pilot is equipped with two type K thermocouples for flame detection:

  • TE-50073A
  • TE-50073B
The thermocouples are installed on the pilot and terminated in a local junction box. Flame detection is used to start an automatic relight.

The ignition system uses propane gas as pilot gas. The self-pressure regulator PCV-50070 is adjusting the gas pressure to 0.48 barg for ignition. The ignition command / control is done through the Local Control Panel located in the package U-0043.

Monitoring of the offloading

The offloading operation is based on a maximum gas rate produced of 5 MMSCFD which is the design capacity of the Vent system.

To monitor the gas rate, the following are installed on the Vent stack:

  • Flow transmitter FIT-50030 ultrasonic type, non-intrusive sending its signal to the FIA-50030. In case of excessive gas produced (Choke valve too much opened) a high flow alarm FIAH-50030 activates at 5 MMSFD to warn operator to reduce the choke valve opening. The FIA-50030 is sending its signal to the flow totalizer FQI-50030.
  • Temperature transmitter TIT-50029 sending its signal to TI-50029, then to FIA-50030 for temperature correction.
  • Pressure transmitter PIT-50031 sending its signal to PI-50031, then to FIA-50030 for pressure correction.

SECTION 4 OPERATING MODES

4.1. OPERATING MODES

Closed Drain Pumps P-0040 A/B can be manual start / stop for maintenance and offloading activities

4.2. NORMAL START-UP

The Closed Drain Pumps P-0040A/B are in duty-standby configuration. The pumps can be started in local or remote. The local/remote and duty/standby selectors are selectable by operator from HMI.

Stand by pump starts when the duty pumps stops for fault. During maintenance activities pumps are set in manual local mode.

4.3. NORMAL OPERATIONS

During normal operations, the Closed Drain Pumps P-0040 A/B are operated in remote from QP2-CR.

4.4. NORMAL SHUTDOWN

The Closed Drain Pumps P-0040 A/B are stopped from DCS at QP2-CR

4.6. EMERGENCY SHUTDOWN

The Closed Drain Pump P-0040 A is stopped by ESD-3.3A
The Closed Drain Pump P-0040 B is stopped by ESD-3.3B
