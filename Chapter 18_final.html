CHAPTER 18 UTILITIES – CHEMICAL STORAGE AND INJECTION

SECTION 1 INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

1.1.1 Purpose

Chemical injection system on WP4 is provided to inject corrosion inhibitor to protect the multiphase pipeline between WP4 and LCP. Injection pumps (2x100%) are provided, with a design flowrate of 3.27 liters/hour.

Tie in point for methanol injection (to minimize risk of hydrate during startup) is provided, but injection skid with a methanol drum is not installed on the platform (following experience from Yadana operation). However, portable methanol injection facilities are recommended to be on hand for start-up with injection recommended as a safeguard against hydrate formation across the choke valves.

1.1.2 System Criticality

The Corrosion Inhibitor injection system is critical to production since it protect the pipeline against corrosion.

The methanol injection system is a critical system to prevent hydrate formation in high-pressure gas streams and it is mainly used for preservation and start-up.

1.1.3 Key components

The system comprises the following main pieces of equipment:

  • Corrosion Inhibitor Storage Tank T-0040
  • Corrosion Inhibitor Tote Tank
  • Corrosion Inhibitor Injection Pumps P-0046 A and B

2.1.1 Corrosion Inhibitor Storage Tank T-0040

Hide image

Corrosion inhibitor is stored in one cylindrical tank T-0040. This tank is located on the Main Deck. Corrosion inhibitor Tote Tank is kept on a platform at EL + 25700 mm above the Corrosion Inhibitor Injection Package.

Corrosion Inhibitor Tote Tank is coupled by hose to hard pipe connections and run down by gravity to the Corrosion Inhibitor Storage Tank (T-0040).

Working capacity of Corrosion Inhibitor Storage Tank T-0040 is 1.21 m3 and covers about 3 weeks of normal dosing rate consumption. Overflow is routed to the drip pan which is connected to the hazardous open drain (OD1).

Corrosion Inhibitor Storage Tank is vented to safe location with flame arrestor and bird screen.

2.1.2 Corrosion Inhibitor Injection Pumps P-0046 A/B

Hide image

The injection pumps (P-0046A/B, simplex pump double diaphragm type) are equipped with Y Strainer at the suction of the pump, pulsation dampener at the discharge of the pump. Pumps and downstream piping are protected by safety valves PSV-50405 / 50408 set at 116.5 barg in case of blocked discharge. The safety valves relieve back to Corrosion Inhibitor storage tank. A calibration pot is provided at the suction of Corrosion Inhibitor Pumps P-0046A/B for calibration and confirmation of injection pump flow rate.

The injection pumps P-0046A/B are in duty-standby configuration. The pumps can be operated in local or remote from QP2-CR PCS. The local/remote and duty/standby selectors are selectable by operator from HMI on the PCS panel of the WP4 Instrument Shelter.

The rated capacity of each Corrosion Inhibitor Injection Pump P-0046A/B is 3.27 l/h. Rates can be varied by adjusting pump stroke and a calibration pot is provided at the suction of pump for calibration and confirmation of injection pump rate. The stand-by pump starts when the duty pump stops for fault.

3.1. CONTROL DESCRIPTION

3.1.1 Corrosion Inhibitor Storage Tank T-0040 - Level Control

The level in the Corrosion Inhibitor Storage Tank T-0040 is monitored by the level transmitter LIT-50402 which sends its signal to LIA-50402. A low level alarm LIAL-50402 activates when the level fall to 500 mm from the bottom (25%). A high level alarm LIAH-50402 activates when the level reaches 1100 mm (69%). The working volume of the Corrosion Inhibitor Storage Tank (T-0040) is between the LIAL to LIAH.

When level high is detected during tank filling, a high level alarm XIA-50402 is generated and a local annunciator will be activated to alert the operator at the filling area.

3.1.2 Corrosion Inhibitor Injection Pumps P-0046 A/B

Hide image

The 2x100% Corrosion Inhibitor Injection Pumps P-0046 A/B are manual stroke adjusting type for setting the chemical injection rate based on main process production rate. The pumps are configured as duty/stand-by.

If the duty pump fails to operate, the stand-by pump will be started automatically. Operator is required to plan the setting of the chemical injection rate by stroke adjustment based on the forecasted production plan.

The pumps can be operated in local or remote from QP2-CR PCS. The local/remote and duty/standby selectors are selectable by operator from HMI on the PCS panel of the WP4 Instrument Shelter.

Not Applicable

the Corrosion Inhibitor Injection pumps P-0046 A/B are stopped from PCS at QP2-CR

SECTION 5 HSE REQUIREMENTS

5.1 HEALTH

5.1.1 Personal Protective Equipment

As several chemicals are used in the system, protective gloves and safety goggles have to be worn at all time.

5.2. SAFETY

5.2.1 Personal Protective Equipment

The Safety Personal Protective equipment should be worn at all time while on site.

5.2.2 Chemical Hazards

Corrosion inhibitor (Refer to MSDS)

Exposure to corrosion inhibitor can lead to:

  • pain, redness and mild burn in case of eye contact,
  • irritation and burn in case of skin contact,
  • headaches, dizziness, nausea, stupor, and other central nervous system effects leading to visual impairment, difficulty breathing, convulsions, and coma in case of inhalation,
  • irritation of the gastrointestinal lining, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain and death in case of ingestion.
