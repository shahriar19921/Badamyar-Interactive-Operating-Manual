CHAPTER 8 SAFETY & CRITICAL SYSTEM – TELECOMMUNICATION SYSTEM

SECTION 1 : INTRODUCTION

1.1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

1.1.1 Purpose

The telecommunications system should be able to achieve the following where applicable:

  • Meet all of the regulatory, mandatory, operational and non-essential telecommunications requirements for the proposed field development;
  • Create a reliable and fully functional integrated telecommunications common infrastructure, to facilitate voice, video and data communications;
  • Ensure that marine and safety requirements are in accordance with international Codes and Standards;
  • Provide telecommunications systems suitable for an offshore environment;
  • Ensure safe and reliable operation by providing proven but latest standards based available technologies (compliant with existing equipment e.g. CCTV);
  • Incorporate scalability and expandability in the telecommunications system throughout all the facilities to meet future requirements;
  • Achieve cost effective and economical design of telecommunications facilities;
  • Ensure compliance to both Local and International standards and regulations;
  • Ensure interoperability with existing Telecom systems on YADANA and ease maintenance activities.

1.1.2 System Criticality

The communication system, as a support to control and supervision for safety operation of the facility, is critical for the protection of personnel and equipment on the platform WP4. Without this system the function of the platform would be inoperable.

Communication by whatever means is critical in the safe operation of the platform whether this is for emergency response in a potential life threatening situation or structurally damaging incident like an unstable process occurrence; i.e. hydrocarbon release, fire or explosion, a maritime incident, Hydrocarbon spillage, collision or man overboard.

1.1.3 Key components

The system comprises the following main subsystems:

  • LAN (Router, Ethernet switch, LAN cabling, data equipment and other accessories).
  • Telephone (Telephone System cabinet, telephone units and other accessories).
  • Crane Radio (Crane Radio control unit, speaker, VHF radio and other accessories.
  • CCTV (CCTV camera, Video Encoder/Decoder and other accessories)
  • Back-up Radio Link. (Radio Indoor Unit, Outdoor Unit, Antenna and associate cable).
  • Wavemeter (Wavemeter radar, Field bus modem, Ex. Certified enclosure, field cabling and other accessories).
  • Fiber Optic Link

2.1.1 General

The primary telecommunications on WP4 is integrated as part of the existing YADANA facilities, thus telecommunications link are established to facilitate communication between WP4 and the existing YADANA facilities.

A submarine fibre optic cable with a suitable high speed / bandwidth communication and a combined power is installed between WP4 and Production Platform (PP) in order to supply power for WP4 and allow to Telephone over Internet Protocol (ToIP) and Camera Closed Circuit Television (CCTV) communication exchange between WP4 and existing YADANA facilities.

The secondary telecommunications, Radio link between WP4 and QP2 platform is established as a back-up link for the submarine fibre optic to enable communications ToIP and CCTV Camera between the WP4 platform and the YADANA facilities. The back-up radio link provided for communications systems will automatically launch in case of any failure on submarine fibre optic to prevent total communication outages.

A radio link shall be capable to provide an efficient, reliable and secure communications for voice, data and video. A single radio link is provisioned to cater for the telecom network.

2.1.2 Telephone System

The telephone shall be made operational via connection to the existing Internet Protocol (Private Automatic) Branch Exchange (IPBX) system installed in the QP2-TER. This shall be facilitated via the LAN system.

The telephone system on WP4 comprises of both indoor and outdoor telephones. Analog telephones required inside the shelter on WP4 is wall mounted type and is explosion-proof certified suitable for use in hazardous areas Class 2. The Eex(d) analog telephones are provided on each deck of WP4 and are installed in an acoustic hood for protection to external noise. The Eex(d) analog telephone sets are equipped with a flashing beacon and sounder in order to improve visibility and sound in specific environment (e.g. noise, fog and dark).

The analog voice gateway is required to provide connectivity to the IP Private Automatic Branch Exchange telephone switch (PABX). This telephone system will allow calls to be made between LCP and WP4 throughout the YADANA facilities (QP2, PP, MCP, WP1, WP2 and WP3 platform), between LCP / WP4 to Yangon (COMPANY office), Onbikwin (COMPANY Pipeline center) and world-wide Public Switched Telephone Network (PSTN).

Access to the local PSTN hop off point in Yangoon via Very Small Aperture Terminal (VSAT) service is also provisioned

2.1.3 Crane Radio System

The existing Marine radio system is designed to cover the entire field area with a range of 30 Nm from the YADANA facilities

Crane cabin has equipment for communication between the crane operators, personnel working on the deck and to supply boats or vessels close to the installation.

Crane radio system is provided in the crane of WP4. The radio operates and functions like a portable radio and is programmed to allow access to all Very High Frequency (VHF) Marine channels normally used by the YADANA field operators.

VHF crane radio system will be equipped with hands free handset integrated with boom microphone or gooseneck microphone and Push-To-Talk (PTT) foot switch to allow crane operator easily to communicate.

Crane radio system consists of control unit (LCD display to channel and volume and loudspeaker) and Ex ‘d’ main unit (VHF Marine radio).

The crane radio system provided for WP4 is designed to have the minimum following functionalities:

  • Provide voice communications between the crane operator, personnel working on deck and supply boat or vessels.
  • Operate of both simplex and duplex.
  • Equipment with all international 5 channels and a minimum of 10 private marine channels.
  • Be fully synthesized and capable of being simply reprogrammed on site.
The Crane radio system shall be designed to meet ATEX requirements for installation and use in hazardous area Class 2.

2.1.4 Closed Circuit Television (CCTV) System

The purpose of the Closed Circuit Television (CCTV) equipment is to allow the monitoring of process facility, operations, and other points of interest on the existing YADANA facilities (QP2 and WP2) for both normal operations and during emergency situations and mustering. The existing central CCTV system is HENRIS IP-based CCTV system provided under the YADANA Subsidence Project.

2.1.5 Local Area Network (LAN) System

The network system design on WP4 has the same functionalities as the existing network system on the YADANA facilities. The following functions are provided as a minimum:

  • Data communication facilities,
  • Telephone services,
  • Providing the interface into the radio system link between WP4 and the YADANA facilities as a back-up communication link.

A reliable office Local Area Network (LAN) System for WP4 shall be provided for data communication services and IP telephone devices as required. Network availability should be 99.99% which is equivalent to 5 minutes downtime/year.

The LAN system is comprised of Cisco Systems hardware/software. The LAN incorporates the use of routing and switching equipment providing wire speed switching performance to the edge.

The LAN comprises of a structured cabling system, allowing for the RJ45 outlets to be used for voice/data connections from either a telephone handset or Personal Computer (PC).

Telecom equipment LAN switches, analog voice gateways and ODF shall be located inside Exd Box on WP4.

2.1.6 Back-up Radio Link System

A Radio Link between WP4 and QP2 platform is established as a back-up link for submarine fiber optic to enable communications between the WP4 platform and the YADANA facilities. The back-up radio link provided for communications systems will automatically launch in case of any failure on submarine fiber optic cable to prevent total communication outages.

The Radio Link will be capable to provide an efficient, reliable and secure communications for voice and data network. A single radio link is provisioned to cater for the telecom network.

The radio equipment is also designed to provide a high availability data network link between WP4 and the existing YADANA facilities using available radio spectrum at the 5.4 GHz unlicensed frequency.

2.1.7 Wavemeter System

The Wavemeter system is installed on WP4, as the data is used during boat landing operation. The existing wavemeter system consists of the following equipment:

  • Main Equipment located in QP2 (TER),
  • Waveradar located within QP2 and WP3,
  • Wavemeter display located in Central Control Room (CCR) at QP2.
The existing waveradar system has been integrated into the Environmental Montoring System (provided by Fugro) installed during Yadana Subsidence project.

The new waveradar is installed near to lowest structure of the platform at the Cellar Deck, to monitor the distance between mean sea level and the lowest structure of the platform. The radar is providing an exceptionally stable and accurate measurement of sea surface profile. The wavemeter network is a standalone network separated from office network.
